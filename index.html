Axel Munthe

A Swedish-born medical doctor and psychiatrist, best known as the author of The Story of San Michele

  • Axel Munthe was born in Oskarshamn, Sweden, his family's home.[1] His family was originally of Flemish descent, and settled in Sweden during the 16th century.
  • Munthe began college in 1874 at Uppsala University.
  • After graduation, Munthe opened a medical practice in Paris, largely catering to the members of the Scandinavian art colony there.
  • In 1887, he moved to Capri, bought the Villa San Michele and began restoration, doing much of the work himself, but also employing local residents, including three brothers and their father.
  • In 1890, running low on money for the project, he opened a practice in Rome which catered to foreign dignitaries as well as the local population. From this point onwards he divided his time between Rome and Capri.
  • In 1892, Munthe was appointed physician to the Swedish royal family.
  • During the First World War, Munthe became a British citizen and served in the ambulance corps.
